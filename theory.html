Equitable Mobility in Central Valley
Ecology / History / Project Theory

The Investigation of Central Valley and Mimi Sheller’s book guided the development of thesis statement.

  • We live in a world of disjointed, uneven terrain. Many species of the world cannot move fairly, securely, and freely. Many species do not have equal access to opportunities, resources, transportation, and are therefore isolated and disconnected. It is this uneven terrain that drives us and informed the approach for this project. For this project, Mimi Sheller’s book Mobility Justice was a guide and inspiration to draw from. In her book, she urges for a new understanding and approach to mobility, one that is fairer and more equal across multiple scales. She states, “Mobility justice is one of the crucial political and ethical issues of our day. It focuses attention on the politics of unequal capabilities for movement, as well as on unequal rights to stay or to dwell in a place.”
  • Through mobility justice we meet our basic needs, we strive, and we push back on societal complacency that fosters exploitation and disconnection.We, the multispecies of the world, are MOBILE BEINGS. Boundaries, borders, and barriers can NEVER REALLY HOLD US BACK. Nothing, whether physical, political, mental, geographic, cartographic, cultural, social, linguistic, technological, economic, imaginary or other forces not yet conceived, are truly capable of halting mobility. We will ALWAYS find a way to connect, united by free-flowing energies, spirits, and souls that keep us moving, living, and striving. We were DESIGNED TO RUN. We were BUILT TO ROAM. We were meant to CHASE OUR DREAMS, wide-eyed as the wind nudges us into STARSCAPES OF BEYOND.Embracing this spirit and energy, we connect ourselves to each other and the world. We promote equitable ease of access and mobility.
  • This project seeks to reconnect disjointed and uneven terrains of society though history (humans), and ecology (multispecies) in the Central Valley of California…The project utilizes State Highway 99 as the backbone for the intervention, seeking to create a localized eco-cultural corridor.(illustrated in the drawing above) Along Highway 99, the project seeks to reinforce or create connections across and along, while weaving neighborhoods back together and creating connections to agriculture. Not only does the project seek to create connectors, but it also creates resting and gathering points throughout the agricultural landscape.
